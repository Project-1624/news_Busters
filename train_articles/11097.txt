Better Serving Our Veterans

I was pleased that the Florida Cabinet unanimously approved the recommendation of the Florida Department of Veterans' Affairs to build their new VA nursing facility in St. Lucie County.



The goal of the new facility is to provide service to aging veterans within a 75-mile radius; that radius on the Treasure Coast is home to over 200,000 veterans, half of which are seniors over the age of 65. Additionally, the new facility is estimated to create nearly 200 new area jobs.

I thank the FDVA, Governor Scott, and the Florida Cabinet for making this a priority as well as our legislative delegation and local officials for their tireless efforts to make this a reality.



Making Sure Our Voices are Heard in Opposition to AAF

In April, Rep. Murphy joined Tequesta officials to view the concerns with the Loxahatchee River train trestle bridge firsthand. The draft Environmental Impact Statement for All Aboard Florida released last week has raised more questions than it answers. While the 75 day public comment period is a marked improvement from the 45 day minimum required by law, it is still inadequate to understand and consider the concerns that countless Floridians have about the proposal.



To have enough time to listen to, understand, and process the concerns of so many concerned Treasure Coast and Palm Beach citizens, I am urging the Federal Railroad Administration to extend the public comment period to at least 90 days.



As a reminder, you can participate in the comment period by sending your comments to FRA at the address below or electronically via: AAF_comments@vhb.com.
